# Sprint Review: Sprint 10

## Summary

Sprint 10 reviewed the backlog slice `Item 28`, `Item 31`, `Item 34`, and `Item 32` (12 out of 15 capacity story points, with a deliberate 3 SP buffer).

- The SDK now supports workflow-level `defaults.run` and permissions shorthand (`read-all`, `write-all`) at both workflow and job scope.
- Identifier-format validation now applies across step IDs, matrix axis names, and `workflow_dispatch` / `workflow_call` identifier-like names using the shared `^[a-zA-Z_][a-zA-Z0-9_-]*$` contract.
- Coverage measurement and CI enforcement now exist for `packages/sdk/src/`, with `bun run coverage` and a 100% line threshold.
- The SDK now supports reusable workflows through `workflow_call` triggers and reusable-workflow caller jobs with `uses`, `with`, and `secrets` / `inherit`.
- Cross-runtime conformance fixtures were added or updated for every SDK-surface item, and `docs/SPEC.md` now documents the new canonical ordering rules.

Review note about repository state:

- This review was performed on `main` after Sprint 10 was merged through sprint closeout PR [#31](https://github.com/moriturus/ghawb.ts/pull/31).
- All four item PRs ([#27](https://github.com/moriturus/ghawb.ts/pull/27), [#28](https://github.com/moriturus/ghawb.ts/pull/28), [#29](https://github.com/moriturus/ghawb.ts/pull/29), [#30](https://github.com/moriturus/ghawb.ts/pull/30)) were merged into the sprint branch with non-implementing review and Product Owner acceptance recorded per item.
- Evidence provenance: review was performed against a clean worktree on `main` at commit `a118cda`.

## Increment Demo

The reviewed increment was demonstrated through the smallest concrete proof already present in repository tests.

1. Reusable-workflow render and validation conformance:

```sh
bun test tests/node/render-conformance.test.ts --test-name-pattern 'workflow_call_and_reusable_job|invalid_workflow_call_and_reusable_job'
```

Important output summary:

- The shared conformance suite passed for both the positive reusable-workflow fixture and the negative validation fixture.
- The positive case proves `workflow_call` trigger rendering with `inputs`, `outputs`, and `secrets`, plus caller-side reusable-workflow job rendering with `uses`, `with`, and `secrets: inherit`.
- The negative case proves the build-time rejection path for unsupported `choice` / `options`, malformed identifier names, and illegal mixing of reusable-workflow jobs with inline `steps` / `runs-on`.

2. Canonical ordering verification for the new surfaces:

```sh
bun test packages/sdk/src/renderer.test.ts --test-name-pattern 'renders reusable workflow jobs in canonical field order|renders workflow_call fields in canonical order'
```

Important output summary:

- The renderer tests passed for both canonical ordering assertions.
- `workflow_call` renders in the documented field order `inputs`, `outputs`, `secrets`.
- Reusable-workflow jobs render in the documented field order `if`, `needs`, `continue-on-error`, `permissions`, `secrets`, `with`, `uses`.

## Review Findings

The sprint delivered the intended increment. No confirmed correctness bugs were found in the reviewed surface.

Confirmed strengths:

- Sprint 10 closed the highest-priority SDK feature gap called out during the post-Sprint-9 GPT review while also hardening validation and verification infrastructure in the same sprint.
- Item 34 materially improved the repository quality contract: the project now measures coverage and fails when the SDK line threshold regresses.
- Item 32 handled the first job-model discriminated union without breaking the existing step-based API surface; compatibility-oriented test updates and cross-runtime fixtures support that boundary.
- Item-level Product Owner acceptance was recorded during sprint execution for all four items, so sprint review did not need to compensate for missing acceptance evidence.
- The sprint closeout path is complete: sprint PR [#31](https://github.com/moriturus/ghawb.ts/pull/31) is merged into `main`.

Residual risks:

- The new reusable-workflow surface increases builder and renderer complexity significantly. The line-coverage gate is now protecting this, but future syntax expansion should continue to prefer small slices over combining multiple job-surface additions in one item.
- `workflow_call` intentionally reuses the `workflow_dispatch` type allowlist while rejecting `choice` and `options`. That contract is now documented and tested, but it is a nuance contributors can misread unless they consult [docs/SPEC.md](../SPEC.md) or the Sprint 10 backlog note.

Open questions:

- None blocking. The delivered increment matches the committed sprint scope and evidence requirements.

## Product Owner Decision

As Product Owner, Aoi Sakamoto accepts the Sprint 10 increment as delivered.

- No backlog reprioritization is required from this review.
- The remaining product backlog order stays `Item 33` → `Item 35` → `Item 30` → `Item 29`.
- The Sprint 10 resequencing decision was validated in practice: placing the coverage gate before `workflow_call` reduced risk for the largest surface expansion in the sprint.
- Future sprint planning should continue to preserve the same rule used here: hardening work may be pulled ahead of broader surface expansion when it reduces verification risk without changing product priority.
