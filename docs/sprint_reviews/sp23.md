# Sprint Review: Sprint 23

## Summary

Sprint 23 delivered all committed backlog items (Items 77 and 78) for a total of 5/5 committed story points. Item PRs #96 and #97 were merged into `sprint-23`, and the sprint closeout PR #98 (`sprint-23` -> `main`) was merged on 2026-04-05.

The increment stayed narrow and architecture-safe: `@ghawb/reusable-workflow-import` now has representative Deno import proof without requiring `--allow-env` for the supported entrypoint slice, and `@ghawb/job-helpers` regained builder-style ergonomics through a generic `JobBuilder.apply(...)` hook without reintroducing helper-specific methods into `@ghawb/sdk`.

No committed sprint item carried over. The active product backlog remains empty after Sprint 23.

## Findings

### No material bugs or acceptance gaps identified in the delivered Sprint 23 increment

The current implementation, sprint backlog record, specification, and public docs align with the Sprint 23 backlog. The review found no missing acceptance slice, no carry-over item, and no confirmed regression in either delivered contract.

### Observation: the `@ghawb/reusable-workflow-import` Deno hardening stayed narrow and package-safe

Item 77 did not broaden the repository's runtime-permission contract. Instead, it isolated the concrete parser-side env probe and hardened the Deno entrypoint path around that bounded behavior. This preserves the repository's preference for explicit, minimal fixes over broad compatibility claims.

### Observation: helper ergonomics improved without reopening the package-boundary decision

Item 78 restored the builder-style authoring feel through a generic `JobBuilder.apply(...)` hook while keeping `nodeCi()` in `@ghawb/job-helpers`. That matches ADR 0006's separation-of-concerns boundary and avoids the previously rejected fallback options of prototype mutation or helper-specific SDK surface area.

### Residual risk: the repository still has no active backlog after Sprint 23

This is not a defect in the delivered increment, but it is a product-flow condition. Future work now depends on fresh backlog intake or discovery rather than on reprioritizing unfinished Sprint 23 scope.

## Product Owner Decision — Aoi Sakamoto

- Sprint 23 delivered all committed scope (5/5 SP, 2/2 items). No committed item requires rework or carry-over.
- No active-backlog reprioritization is needed because no active backlog items remain after Sprint 23 closeout.
- The repository should preserve the hardening-first product stance validated by Sprint 23: compatibility and contract fixes should continue to outrank convenience-oriented surface growth when new ambiguity is discovered.
- The `@ghawb/job-helpers` package boundary remains the correct home for high-level helper sequences, and future helper ergonomics should continue to build on the generic `JobBuilder.apply(...)` pattern rather than widening `@ghawb/sdk` with helper-specific methods.
- The next planning input should come from new backlog intake or product discovery rather than from reopening Sprint 23 scope.
